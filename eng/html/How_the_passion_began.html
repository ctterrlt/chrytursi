My Chess Journey

My passion for chess was born almost by chance, back in fifth grade. Every week, a chess instructor would come to our school to give lessons. Unlike most of my classmates, I had never played before. Yet, in no time just after the first two lessons I had become the strongest player in the class. I had beaten all my peers and started playing regularly with the instructor, even though he went quite easy on me.

Back then, my favorite checkmates were the ones using two rooks or the queen and rook combination — simple, yet highly effective. If I had to estimate my playing strength at the time, I'd say I was around 650 Elo.

Despite the coach encouraging me to join a chess club and play in tournaments, I refused and completely stopped playing. For years, chess remained just a memory.

Everything changed during my first year of high school, when — thanks to TikTok bringing chess back into the spotlight — I started playing a casual game once a week with some classmates. I also took part in a school tournament, but I wasn’t very well-trained and the results weren’t great.

The real turning point came in my third year. After switching classes, I met new classmates who were much more experienced and passionate about the game. Thanks to them, I began training consistently again. Before the regional school championship, my estimated rating had risen to about 1100 Elo.

Since then, I haven’t stopped. Today I keep playing and training with dedication. I take inspiration from top chess content creators like Levy Rozman (better known as GothamChess), several chess instructors on TikTok, and above all Candidate Master Redon Sheta, with whom I’ve played several live games. He has been a true mentor in my chess improvement.

What’s next? On May 25, 2025, I’ll be playing in an important tournament, facing off against several 1st Category players and Candidate Masters. I feel confident and ready to give it my all.
